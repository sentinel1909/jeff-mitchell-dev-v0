About Me



Hello, I'm Jeff. I'm a professional engineer by day and an up-and-coming software developer by night. I live in Vancouver, Canada. Welcome to my corner of the World Wide Web.


I grew up around computers and cut my teeth coding in SMARTBasic on the Coleco ADAM computer system. After a work term with IBM in the fall of 1992, I fell in love with the PC world and was gifted a computer for my birthday in 1993. In University, I learned how to code in Turbo Pascal, with a little C thrown in for good measure. In the mid to late nineties, I embraced the World Wide Web and made my first very simple websites. Most of what I make for the web now looks like it came from that era, but I digress...


My day job doesn't involve software development, so I drifted away from programming for a long number of years. At the end of 2020, in the dark hours of COVID, I decided I wanted to pick up coding again, mainly to skill stack and make myself a more capable person.


I started with C++, but quickly realized (I sort of already knew) that it was a harsh mistress and I would never be truly confident with it. I also want to create things to view and consume on the web. As a fledgling, solo-developer, I needed something better...and I knew I just didn't want to be the billionth person learning JavaScript. Enter Rust. Rust is a language which enables people to build reliable and efficient software. I fell in love almost immediately with this notion. Rust, combined with traditional web tech, enables me to develop things and help people. I hope you enjoy my writing and that it benefits you in your own journey!
